Club Information

Roller hockey at Arizona State University is offered as a student-run club sport that operates under ASU's sport clubs program. The club provides athletes with the opportunity to compete at a high level while balancing academics and campus life at an established university. The club has two distinguished teams: Division 1 and AA. Both teams participate in the National Collegiate Roller Hockey Association (NCRHA) which maintains all university roller hockey and the Western Collegiate Roller Hockey League (WCRHL) which hosts regional competitions. Often opponents include Grand Canyon University and the University of Arizona. The club practices and competes at Solid Ground Arena, an off-campus rink located in Peoria, Arizona. Led by Nick Boyarski and George Godinez, the club's renowned coaching staff ensures that players receive the highest quality training and development. Arizona State University holds a strong reputation for academics across their four major campuses in the valley. With a strong student-athelete emphasis, roller hockey thrives as a part of the ASU club sports community while also excelling academically.

Accomplishments

  • Division 1: 5-time WCRHL Regional Champions
  • Division AA: 11-time WCRHL Regional Champions
  • Division 1: 2010 NCRHA National Championship Appearance
  • Division AA: 2013 NCRHA National Championship Appearance

News

  • Division 1 and AA are both headed to NCRHA Nationals in Springfield, Missouri, April 14 through April 19!
  • Division 1 finished the regular season with a 15-9-0 record and AA finished the regular season with a 13-10-0 record.
  • Jonathan Leishman led Division 1 with a 22-25-47 stat line in 20 games played.
  • Ty Wilson led AA with a 43-22-65 stat line in 18 games played.
